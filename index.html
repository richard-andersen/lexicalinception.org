Lexical Inception

bot-speak

/ˈbɒtˌspiːk/ (BOT-speek)

: the subtle adoption of AI-favored vocabulary into human conversation, making the source of the language recognizable to anyone who spends time talking to bots

hallucinatory alignment

/həˈluːsɪnəˌtɔːri əˈlaɪnmənt/ (huh-LOO-sih-nuh-tor-ee uh-LINE-muhnt)

: when language, tone, or reasoning sounds well-aligned, coherent, and authoritative, but is actually stitched together from pattern completion rather than grounded understanding or intent

the alignment is aesthetic rather than epistemic

lexical inception

/ˈlɛksɪkəl ɪnˈsɛpʃən/ (LEK-si-kul in-SEP-shun)

: the deliberate seeding of specific words or phrases into an audience’s vocabulary such that they are later used as if self-generated

distinguished from lexical convergence, which is passive and emergent

dictionary cosplay

/ˈdɪkʃəˌnɛri ˈkɒsˌpleɪ/ (DIK-shuh-nair-ee KOSS-play)

: the performative use of dictionary or academic conventions to project rigor or authority without adding substantive clarity or meaning

includes unnecessary part-of-speech labels, phonetic spellings, or formal markers that signal rigor or provenance without aiding in understanding
